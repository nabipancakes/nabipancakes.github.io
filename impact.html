Revitalizing Oakridge Elementary's Library

When we first visited Oakridge Elementary, their library shelves were sparse and outdated. Through our targeted book drive, we collected over 1,200 new and gently used books specifically selected to reflect the diverse backgrounds of Oakridge students.

"The transformation has been incredible. Students who rarely visited the library before are now regular visitors, excited to explore new stories," says Principal Marcia Greene. "The Book Diaries didn't just donate books—they helped us create a vibrant reading culture."

Summer Reading Program Bridges the Gap

Research shows that many students experience "summer slide"—losing up to two months of reading skills during summer break. Our Summer Reading Adventure program works to prevent this learning loss by keeping students engaged with books all summer long.

Last summer, 237 students participated in our 8-week program, collectively reading over 3,500 books. Teachers reported that participants showed stronger reading skills at the start of the school year compared to non-participants.

Building Community Through Shared Reading

Our neighborhood book clubs have become vital community hubs where people from diverse backgrounds come together through literature. What started as a single monthly meeting at the local community center has grown into a network of 12 clubs meeting regularly across the city.

These groups do more than discuss books—they build lasting friendships, support local authors, and create intergenerational connections that strengthen the fabric of our community.
